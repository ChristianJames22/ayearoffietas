Higantes Festival

Angono, Rizal – November 23

The Higantes Festival is a unique celebration in Angono, Rizal, held annually on November 23 in honor of Saint Clement, the town's patron saint and the patron of fishermen.

Its highlight is the parade of giant papier-mâché figures known as “higantes,” which stand up to 12 feet tall. These figures represent Spanish-era landlords and have become a symbol of freedom and local creativity.

The festival also features processions, fluvial parades, street dancing, and the famous “basaan” (splashing of water for blessings). Higantes is a vibrant blend of history, satire, and art.
