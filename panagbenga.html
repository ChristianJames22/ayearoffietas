Panagbenga, which means "season of blooming," is an annual flower festival celebrated in Baguio City throughout the month of February. It honors the city's floral heritage and the resilience of its people after the 1990 Luzon earthquake.

The celebration is known for its stunning grand float parade filled with fresh flowers, colorful street dancing inspired by the region's indigenous culture, and a month-long exhibition of local creativity and talent.

Panagbenga Festival has become one of the most anticipated tourist events in the Philippines, offering a cool-weathered, flower-filled escape to the summer capital.
